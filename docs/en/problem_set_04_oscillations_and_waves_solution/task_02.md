# Problem 2 – Energy of a Harmonic Oscillator

## Given

A harmonic oscillator has the following parameters:

- Mass: $m = 1 \,\text{kg}$
- Spring constant: $k = 100 \,\text{N/m}$
- Initial displacement: $x(0) = 2 \,\text{m}$
- Initial velocity: $v(0) = 1 \,\text{m/s}$

---

## Find

1. The natural angular frequency $\omega_0$
2. The total mechanical energy $E$
3. The displacement $x$ at which the kinetic energy is equal to $50\%$ of the total energy

---

## Solution

### 1. Natural frequency

The natural angular frequency of a harmonic oscillator is

$$
\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{100}{1}} = 10 \,\text{rad/s}
$$

Also, the ordinary frequency is

$$
f = \frac{\omega_0}{2\pi}
$$

$$
f = \frac{10}{2\pi} \approx 1.59 \,\text{Hz}
$$

**Answer:**

$$
\omega_0 = 10 \,\text{rad/s}, \qquad f \approx 1.59 \,\text{Hz}
$$

---

### 2. Total mechanical energy

The total mechanical energy is

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}kx^2
$$

Using the initial values:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1)(1^2) + \frac{1}{2}(100)(2^2)
$$

$$
E = 0.5 + 200 = 200.5 \,\text{J}
$$

**Answer:**

$$
E = 200.5 \,\text{J}
$$

---

### 3. Displacement when kinetic energy is 50% of total energy

If kinetic energy is $50\%$ of total energy, then potential energy is also $50\%$ of total energy:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}E
$$

Since

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
$$

we write

$$
\frac{1}{2}kx^2 = \frac{1}{2}E
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\frac{1}{2}(100)x^2 = \frac{1}{2}(200.5)
$$

$$
50x^2 = 100.25
$$

$$
x^2 = 2.005
$$

$$
x = \pm \sqrt{2.005} \approx \pm 1.42 \,\text{m}
$$

**Answer:**

$$
x \approx \pm 1.42 \,\text{m}
$$

---

## Final Answers

- Natural angular frequency:

$$
\omega_0 = 10 \,\text{rad/s}
$$

- Ordinary frequency:

$$
f \approx 1.59 \,\text{Hz}
$$

- Total mechanical energy:

$$
E = 200.5 \,\text{J}
$$

- Displacement when $K = 50\%E$:

$$
x \approx \pm 1.42 \,\text{m}
$$
