# Problem 2 – Energy of a Harmonic Oscillator

## Given

- $m = 1 \ \text{kg}$
- $k = 100 \ \text{N/m}$
- $x_0 = 2 \ \text{m}$
- $v_0 = 1 \ \text{m/s}$

---

## 1. Natural frequency

Formula:

$$
\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

Calculation:

$$
\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{100}{1}} = 10 \ \text{rad/s}
$$

$$
f = \frac{\omega_0}{2\pi} = \frac{10}{2\pi} \approx 1.59 \ \text{Hz}
$$

**Answer:**
- $\omega_0 = 10 \ \text{rad/s}$
- $f \approx 1.59 \ \text{Hz}$

---

## 2. Total energy

Formula:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + \frac{1}{2}kx^2
$$

Calculation:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1)(1^2) + \frac{1}{2}(100)(2^2)
$$

$$
E = 0.5 + 200 = 200.5 \ \text{J}
$$

**Answer:**

$$
E = 200.5 \ \text{J}
$$

---

## 3. When $K = 50\%E$

Since:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 = 0.5E
$$

$$
50x^2 = 100.25
$$

$$
x^2 = 2.005
$$

$$
x = \pm 1.42 \ \text{m}
$$

**Answer:**

$$
x \approx \pm 1.42 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Conclusion

- System oscillates with $\omega_0 = 10 \ \text{rad/s}$
- Total energy is $200.5 \ \text{J}$
- At $x \approx \pm 1.42 \ \text{m}$, kinetic energy is 50% of total
