# Problem 6

## Given

Resistors:

$$
R_1 = r
$$

$$
R_2 = 2r
$$

$$
R_3 = r
$$

$$
R_4 = r
$$

$$
R_5 = 6r
$$

Find:

- Equivalent resistance
- Current through each resistor

---

# Solution

Let the voltage between points \(A\) and \(B\) be:

$$
U
$$

Using Kirchhoff’s laws for points \(C\) and \(D\):

$$
V_C = \frac{28U}{43}
$$

$$
V_D = \frac{22U}{43}
$$

Total current:

$$
I = I_1 + I_3
$$

$$
I_1 = \frac{U - V_C}{r}
$$

$$
I_1 = \frac{U - \frac{28U}{43}}{r}
$$

$$
I_1 = \frac{15U}{43r}
$$

$$
I_3 = \frac{U - V_D}{r}
$$

$$
I_3 = \frac{U - \frac{22U}{43}}{r}
$$

$$
I_3 = \frac{21U}{43r}
$$

So,

$$
I = \frac{15U}{43r} + \frac{21U}{43r}
$$

$$
I = \frac{36U}{43r}
$$

Equivalent resistance:

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{U}{I}
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{U}{\frac{36U}{43r}}
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{43r}{36}
$$

---

# Currents through each resistor

$$
I_1 = \frac{15U}{43r}
$$

$$
I_2 = \frac{V_C}{2r}
$$

$$
I_2 = \frac{14U}{43r}
$$

$$
I_3 = \frac{21U}{43r}
$$

$$
I_4 = \frac{V_D}{r}
$$

$$
I_4 = \frac{22U}{43r}
$$

$$
I_5 = \frac{V_C - V_D}{6r}
$$

$$
I_5 = \frac{U}{43r}
$$

---

# Conclusion

Equivalent resistance:

$$
\boxed{R_{eq} = \frac{43r}{36}}
$$

Currents:

$$
\boxed{I_1 = \frac{15U}{43r}}
$$

$$
\boxed{I_2 = \frac{14U}{43r}}
$$

$$
\boxed{I_3 = \frac{21U}{43r}}
$$

$$
\boxed{I_4 = \frac{22U}{43r}}
$$

$$
\boxed{I_5 = \frac{U}{43r}}
$$
