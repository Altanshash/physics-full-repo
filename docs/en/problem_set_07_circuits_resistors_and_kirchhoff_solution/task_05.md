# Problem 5

## Given

$$
R_1 = 5\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_2 = 10\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_3 = 10\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_4 = 10\ \Omega
$$

Find:

- Equivalent resistance
- Current through ammeter \(A\)

---

# Solution

The two lower resistors are connected in parallel:

$$
R_{34} = \frac{10 \times 10}{10 + 10}
$$

$$
R_{34} = \frac{100}{20}
$$

$$
R_{34} = 5\ \Omega
$$

Now this resistance is connected in series with the \(5\ \Omega\) resistor:

$$
R_{134} = 5 + 5
$$

$$
R_{134} = 10\ \Omega
$$

The upper resistor \(10\ \Omega\) is connected in parallel with this branch:

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{10 \times 10}{10 + 10}
$$

$$
R_{eq} = \frac{100}{20}
$$

$$
R_{eq} = 5\ \Omega
$$

---

# Ammeter Current

The circuit splits into two equal branches:

- upper branch: \(10\ \Omega\)
- lower branch: \(10\ \Omega\)

Therefore the current divides equally.

If total current is:

$$
I = \frac{U}{5}
$$

Then the current through the ammeter branch is:

$$
I_A = \frac{I}{2}
$$

$$
I_A = \frac{U}{10}
$$

---

# Conclusion

Equivalent resistance:

$$
\boxed{R_{eq} = 5\ \Omega}
$$

Current through ammeter:

$$
\boxed{I_A = \frac{U}{10}}
$$
