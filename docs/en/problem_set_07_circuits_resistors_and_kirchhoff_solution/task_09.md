# Problem 9

## Given

$$
\mathcal{E}_1 = 9\ V
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_2 = 4.5\ V
$$

$$
R_1 = 10\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_2 = 20\ \Omega
$$

$$
r_1 = 1\ \Omega
$$

$$
r_2 = 1\ \Omega
$$

Find the current through the ammeter.

---

# Solution

The circuit forms one closed loop.

Total resistance:

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = R_1 + R_2 + r_1 + r_2
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 10 + 20 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 32\ \Omega
$$

The batteries are connected in opposite directions:

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = \mathcal{E}_1 - \mathcal{E}_2
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = 9 - 4.5
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = 4.5\ V
$$

Using Ohm’s law:

$$
I = \frac{\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}}}{R_{\text{eq}}}
$$

$$
I = \frac{4.5}{32}
$$

$$
I = 0.1406\ A
$$

---

# Conclusion

The current through the ammeter is:

$$
\boxed{I_A \approx 0.141\ A}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{I_A \approx 141\ mA}
$$
