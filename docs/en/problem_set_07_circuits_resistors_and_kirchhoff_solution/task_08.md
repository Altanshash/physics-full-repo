# Problem 8

## Given

$$
\mathcal{E}_1 = 4.5\ V
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_2 = 1.5\ V
$$

$$
R_1 = 20\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_2 = 40\ \Omega
$$

$$
R_3 = 60\ \Omega
$$

$$
r_1 = 1\ \Omega
$$

$$
r_2 = 1\ \Omega
$$

Find the current through the ammeter.

## Solution

All resistances are connected in series:

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + r_1 + r_2
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 20 + 40 + 60 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 122\ \Omega
$$

The batteries are connected in opposite directions:

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = \mathcal{E}_1 - \mathcal{E}_2
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = 4.5 - 1.5
$$

$$
\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}} = 3\ V
$$

Using Ohm’s law:

$$
I = \frac{\mathcal{E}_{\text{eq}}}{R_{\text{eq}}}
$$

$$
I = \frac{3}{122}
$$

$$
I \approx 0.0246\ A
$$

## Conclusion

The current through the ammeter is:

$$
\boxed{I_A \approx 0.025\ A}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{I_A \approx 25\ mA}
$$
