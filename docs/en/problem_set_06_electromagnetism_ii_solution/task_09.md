# Problem 9 – Ideal vs. Real Transformer

## Problem Statement

Explain how a transformer works, what its ideal properties are, and what reality looks like.

Answer the following questions:

1. How does a transformer work?
2. Where does the secondary voltage come from?
3. What are the losses in a transformer?
4. What are the typical values of the turns ratio?
5. What is transformer efficiency?

---

# Solution

## 1. Principle of Transformer Operation

A transformer is an electrical device that transfers energy between two circuits using electromagnetic induction.

It consists of:

- a primary coil with $N_1$ turns,
- a secondary coil with $N_2$ turns,
- and a magnetic core.

An alternating current in the primary coil creates a changing magnetic flux:

$$
\Phi(t)
$$

This changing magnetic flux passes through the secondary coil and induces an EMF according to Faraday’s law:

$$
\mathcal{E} = -N\frac{d\Phi}{dt}
$$

Thus, electrical energy is transferred from the primary circuit to the secondary circuit through the magnetic field.

---

## 2. Origin of Secondary Voltage

The secondary voltage appears because the magnetic flux generated by the primary coil changes with time.

The changing magnetic field induces voltage in the secondary winding:

$$
U_2 = -N_2\frac{d\Phi}{dt}
$$

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{U_2}{U_1} = \frac{N_2}{N_1}
$$

where:

- $U_1$ is the primary voltage,
- $U_2$ is the secondary voltage,
- $N_1$ is the number of turns in the primary coil,
- $N_2$ is the number of turns in the secondary coil.

If:

$$
N_2 > N_1
$$

the transformer is step-up.

If:

$$
N_2 < N_1
$$

the transformer is step-down.

---

## 3. Ideal Transformer

An ideal transformer has:

- no resistance in the windings,
- no energy loss,
- no magnetic leakage,
- perfect magnetic coupling,
- infinite core permeability.

For an ideal transformer:

$$
P_1 = P_2
$$

or:

$$
U_1I_1 = U_2I_2
$$

Efficiency:

$$
\eta = 100\%
$$

---

## 4. Real Transformer and Losses

Real transformers are not perfect.

They have several types of losses.

---

### 4.1 Copper Losses

Current flowing through winding resistance produces Joule heating:

$$
P_{\text{Cu}} = I^2R
$$

These losses increase with current.

---

### 4.2 Core Losses

The magnetic core experiences:

### Hysteresis losses

Energy is lost during repeated magnetization and demagnetization.

### Eddy current losses

Changing magnetic flux induces currents inside the iron core.

These currents produce heat.

To reduce eddy currents, laminated cores are used.

---

### 4.3 Magnetic Leakage

Not all magnetic flux from the primary winding reaches the secondary winding.

Some flux leaks into surrounding space.

---

### 4.4 Mechanical and Acoustic Losses

Small losses also occur due to:

- vibration,
- magnetostriction,
- sound.

---

## 5. Turns Ratio

The turns ratio is:

$$
k = \frac{N_2}{N_1}
$$

Voltage ratio:

$$
\frac{U_2}{U_1} = k
$$

Current ratio:

$$
\frac{I_2}{I_1} = \frac{1}{k}
$$

Typical examples:

### Step-down transformer

$$
N_1 = 1000,\quad N_2 = 100
$$

$$
k = 0.1
$$

### Step-up transformer

$$
N_1 = 100,\quad N_2 = 2000
$$

$$
k = 20
$$

---

## 6. Transformer Efficiency

Efficiency is:

$$
\eta = \frac{P_{\text{out}}}{P_{\text{in}}}\times100\%
$$

Real transformers usually have high efficiency.

Typical values:

- small transformers:
  
$$
\eta \approx 80\%-90\%
$$

- power transformers:
  
$$
\eta \approx 95\%-99\%
$$

Large industrial transformers can exceed:

$$
99\%
$$

---

# Final Conclusion

A transformer transfers electrical energy through electromagnetic induction.

A changing current in the primary coil creates changing magnetic flux, which induces voltage in the secondary coil.

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{U_2}{U_1} = \frac{N_2}{N_1}
$$

and:

$$
P_1 = P_2
$$

Real transformers experience losses due to:

- winding resistance,
- hysteresis,
- eddy currents,
- magnetic leakage.

The efficiency of real transformers is usually very high:

$$
\eta \approx 95\%-99\%
$$

depending on transformer size and design.
