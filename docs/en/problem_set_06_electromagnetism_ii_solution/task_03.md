# Problem 3 – Magnetic Moment of a Loop

## Problem Statement

We have a conducting loop with $N$ turns and area $S$.  
A current $I$ flows through the loop.

The loop is located in a uniform magnetic field:

$$
\vec{B}
$$

Answer the following questions:

1. Define the magnetic moment $\vec{\mu}$ of the loop.
2. Determine the torque $\vec{M}$.
3. For what angle is the torque maximum?
4. Determine the potential energy $U$.
5. Indicate the stable and unstable positions.

---

## Given

$$
N = \text{number of turns}
$$

$$
S = \text{area of the loop}
$$

$$
I = \text{current}
$$

$$
\vec{B} = \text{uniform magnetic field}
$$

$$
\theta = \text{angle between } \vec{\mu} \text{ and } \vec{B}
$$

---

## Solution

## 1. Magnetic Moment

The magnetic moment of a current loop is:

$$
\vec{\mu} = NIS\vec{n}
$$

where:

- $N$ is the number of turns
- $I$ is the current
- $S$ is the area
- $\vec{n}$ is the unit vector perpendicular to the loop surface

The magnitude is:

$$
\mu = NIS
$$

---

## 2. Torque on the Loop

The torque acting on a magnetic dipole in a magnetic field is:

$$
\vec{M} = \vec{\mu} \times \vec{B}
$$

The magnitude of torque is:

$$
M = \mu B \sin\theta
$$

Substitute $\mu = NIS$:

$$
M = NISB\sin\theta
$$

---

## 3. Maximum Torque

Torque is:

$$
M = NISB\sin\theta
$$

The maximum value of $\sin\theta$ is:

$$
\sin\theta = 1
$$

Therefore:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

So the torque is maximum when $\vec{\mu}$ is perpendicular to $\vec{B}$.

Maximum torque:

$$
M_{\max} = NISB
$$

---

## 4. Potential Energy

The potential energy of a magnetic dipole in a magnetic field is:

$$
U = -\vec{\mu}\cdot\vec{B}
$$

Using the dot product:

$$
U = -\mu B\cos\theta
$$

Substitute $\mu = NIS$:

$$
U = -NISB\cos\theta
$$

---

## 5. Stable and Unstable Positions

### Stable position

The stable position occurs when potential energy is minimum.

$$
U = -\mu B\cos\theta
$$

Minimum energy occurs when:

$$
\cos\theta = 1
$$

So:

$$
\theta = 0^\circ
$$

This means:

$$
\vec{\mu} \parallel \vec{B}
$$

The magnetic moment is in the same direction as the magnetic field.

---

### Unstable position

The unstable position occurs when potential energy is maximum.

Maximum energy occurs when:

$$
\cos\theta = -1
$$

So:

$$
\theta = 180^\circ
$$

This means:

$$
\vec{\mu} \text{ is opposite to } \vec{B}
$$

---

## Final Conclusion

The magnetic moment of the loop is:

$$
\vec{\mu} = NIS\vec{n}
$$

The torque is:

$$
\vec{M} = \vec{\mu} \times \vec{B}
$$

and its magnitude is:

$$
M = NISB\sin\theta
$$

The torque is maximum at:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

The potential energy is:

$$
U = -NISB\cos\theta
$$

The stable position is:

$$
\theta = 0^\circ
$$

The unstable position is:

$$
\theta = 180^\circ
$$
