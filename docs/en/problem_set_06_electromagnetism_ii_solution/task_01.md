# Problem 1 – Lorentz Force

## Given

$$
\vec{B} = (0,0,1)\ \text{T}
$$

$$
\vec{v} = (2,3,0)\ \text{m/s}
$$

$$
q = 1\,\text{mC} = 1 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{C}
$$

---

## 1. Lorentz Force

The Lorentz force is:

$$
\vec{F} = q (\vec{v} \times \vec{B})
$$

### Cross product:

$$
\vec{v} \times \vec{B} =
\begin{vmatrix}
\hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\
2 & 3 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= (3, -2, 0)
$$

### Force:

$$
\vec{F} = (3 \times 10^{-3}, -2 \times 10^{-3}, 0)\ \text{N}
$$

---

## 2. Equation of Motion

Newton's second law:

$$
m \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = \vec{F}
$$

Since the magnetic force is perpendicular to velocity, the motion is **circular** in the XY plane.

---

## 3. Magnitude of Force

$$
|\vec{F}| = \sqrt{(3 \times 10^{-3})^2 + (-2 \times 10^{-3})^2}
$$

$$
|\vec{F}| = \sqrt{13} \times 10^{-3}
$$

$$
|\vec{F}| \approx 3.61 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{N}
$$

---

## 4. Work Done by Magnetic Force

$$
W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} = 0
$$

Magnetic force does **no work**.

---

## 5. Radius of Trajectory

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

Speed:

$$
v = \sqrt{2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{13}
$$

Substitute:

$$
r = \frac{0.01 \cdot \sqrt{13}}{(1 \times 10^{-3}) \cdot 1}
$$

$$
r = 10\sqrt{13}
$$

$$
r \approx 36.1\ \text{m}
$$

---

## 6. Effect of Doubling Magnetic Field

$$
r \propto \frac{1}{B}
$$

If $B$ doubles:

$$
r \rightarrow \frac{r}{2}
$$

---

## Final Answer

- Lorentz force:

$$
\vec{F} = (3 \times 10^{-3}, -2 \times 10^{-3}, 0)\ \text{N}
$$

- Magnitude:

$$
|\vec{F}| \approx 3.61 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{N}
$$

- Motion: **circular**

- Magnetic force does **no work**

- Radius:

$$
r \approx 36.1\ \text{m}
$$

- If magnetic field doubles → radius becomes **2 times smaller**
